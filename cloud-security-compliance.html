The cloud challenge

Cloud adoption grows quickly. Governance often does not.

Without clear ownership, standards and monitoring, cloud environments can become difficult to secure, manage and audit.

01

Configuration drift

Security settings may change over time without review, approval or documentation.

02

Unclear ownership

Teams may not know who is responsible for identities, data, applications or risk decisions.

03

Excessive privilege

Administrative roles and user access may remain broader than necessary.

04

Weak evidence

Organizations may struggle to demonstrate policies, reviews and control operation during audits.

05

Inconsistent controls

Different workloads may be protected using different standards or incomplete baselines.

06

Limited visibility

Logs, alerts and reporting may not provide enough information to support security decisions.

Governance framework

Four pillars for a more controlled cloud environment.

A practical governance model connects accountability, policy, technical controls and continuous review.

1Accountability

Define owners, administrators, approvers and risk decision-makers.

2Standards

Document minimum requirements for identity, data, devices and configurations.

3Controls

Implement technical and procedural safeguards aligned with business risk.

4Assurance

Review evidence, exceptions, access and security posture regularly.
