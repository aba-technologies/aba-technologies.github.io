Migration challenges

A successful migration requires more than moving data.

Business continuity, permissions, security, user readiness and governance must all be handled carefully.

01

Downtime and disruption

Poor planning can interrupt email, file access and daily business operations.

02

Data and permission gaps

Files may migrate without the correct ownership, sharing or access structure.

03

Security exposure

Misconfigured identities, external sharing and legacy access can create risk.

04

User adoption problems

Employees need clear communication and guidance before the new platform goes live.

05

Hidden dependencies

Applications, DNS, devices and workflows may rely on the existing environment.

06

Incomplete validation

Without structured testing, missing data or configuration issues may appear later.

Our migration methodology

A controlled process from discovery to stabilization.

Every stage is designed to reduce uncertainty and protect business continuity.

1

Assess

Review users, licences, data volumes, applications, DNS, permissions and dependencies.

2

Design

Define migration batches, target architecture, coexistence, security and rollback considerations.

3

Pilot

Test a controlled set of users and workloads before the broader migration begins.

4

Migrate

Execute email, file, identity and collaboration migration according to the approved plan.

5

Validate

Check data, access, mail flow, sharing, devices and critical business workflows.

6

Stabilize

Resolve post-migration issues, transfer knowledge and provide user support.
