The security challenge

Your digital workplace is a major target for cyber threats.

Cloud productivity platforms contain identities, communications, files and business-critical data. Security must be designed across the entire environment.

01

Account compromise

Weak passwords, missing MFA and risky sign-ins can expose sensitive business systems.

02

Phishing and email threats

Malicious links, impersonation and business email compromise remain common attack methods.

03

Unmanaged devices

Personal or non-compliant endpoints can access company data without sufficient control.

04

Excessive access

Users and administrators may retain privileges they no longer require.

05

Uncontrolled sharing

Files may be shared externally without the right visibility, restrictions or retention rules.

06

Limited monitoring

Security events can go unnoticed when alerts, logs and response processes are not configured.

Zero Trust approach

Never trust by default. Verify every access decision.

A practical Zero Trust model considers identity, device health, application context, data sensitivity and ongoing monitoring.

1Identity

Verify who is requesting access.

2Device

Check whether the device is trusted and compliant.

3Application

Control which apps can access business data.

4Data

Protect information based on sensitivity and context.

5Monitoring

Review activity, alerts and signs of compromise.
